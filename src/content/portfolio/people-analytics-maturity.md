Most organisations know they want to "do more with people data," but very few have a clear picture of where they actually are on that journey or what the realistic next step looks like. The People Analytics Maturity Framework is a diagnostic tool I developed through work with Ikona Analytics to help organisations assess their current capabilities across five dimensions: data infrastructure, analytical capability, stakeholder integration, ethical governance, and strategic alignment. Each dimension is evaluated independently because maturity is rarely uniform — an organisation might have sophisticated data pipelines but almost no capability to translate analytical outputs into business decisions.

The framework is deliberately not a linear maturity model. The traditional "Level 1 to Level 5" models imply a single path and a single destination, which doesn't reflect how people analytics functions actually evolve. Instead, this framework identifies capability clusters and the dependencies between them, helping organisations understand which investments will unlock the most value given their current profile. For example, investing in advanced predictive modelling before establishing basic data quality governance is a pattern that wastes budget and erodes stakeholder trust.

The output is a diagnostic profile with prioritised recommendations — not a score. The goal is to give PA leaders and their executive sponsors a shared vocabulary for discussing where they are, where they need to be, and what the realistic path between those two points looks like.
